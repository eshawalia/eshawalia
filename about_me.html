About Me

My name is Esha Ahluwalia and I’m 26 years young! I was born and raised in Dubai and lived there until I graduated high school from Jumeirah College. After that I moved to Boston where I did my undergraduate degree in Marketing at Northeastern University - go Huskies! At the same time my mother moved to London so that was my new homebase and where I went during university holidays.

I was really excited to live in London because I hadn’t been to many European countries. However, I couldn’t explore Europe much because of the pandemic - but I was able to visit Italy with my mother, right before COVID-19. We visited ten different cities in Italy in a span of two weeks and it is till date my favorite holiday!

After I graduated from college, I worked in Boston for a year at a company called John Hancock and then eventually moved to Dehradun, India. Living in India was a culture shock at first because even though I’m Indian I had never lived in India before. Eventually, as I acclimated, I really loved living there because my mother had also retired and moved back and I have a lot of family in Dehradun as well, so being close to my family and my culture felt really nice.

Whilst living in India I completed an internship at a company called Mom’s Choice and worked on applying for Graduate School. Even though, I had a Marketing background I really wanted to make a career switch to UX/UI Design so I applied for Graduate programs in this field. I worked hard on crafting my applications for several months and luckily it paid off because I was accepted into several amazing colleges:

  • Pratt Institute
  • New York University
  • Arizona State University
  • University of Michigan
  • University of Washington
  • School of Visual Arts, New York
  • University of Maryland
  • Northeastern University
  • DePaul University

As you can imagine it was really hard to decide which college to attend, but I finally decided on Pratt Institute’s MS IXD program because of the following reasons:

  1. They gave me a generous scholarship.
  2. The curriculum and professors were fantastic.
  3. The school is in New York so there are many job and networking opportunities.
  4. Pratt has a strong alumni network.

Now that I’ve moved to New York and I’m attending Pratt Institute, I can wholeheartedly say that I made the right decision because I’m enjoying living in New York and I’m loving my Pratt student experience.
